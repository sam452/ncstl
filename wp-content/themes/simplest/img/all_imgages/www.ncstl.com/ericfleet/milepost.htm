Footnotes:

(A) NC&STL Rwy. Winter 1955 Time Table No. 2 Atlanta, Nashville, Bruceton and Memphis, pg. 9.
(B) An icon "" is 1913 NC&STL track diagram & profile (updated circa 1930's) available. See legend for the abbreviations and symbols.
(C) Method of Operation:
      The Bruceton to Memphis segment of the Paducah & Memphis Division didn't receive any more advanced method of operation than timetabale/train order       operation until CTC installation was completed around September 1954.
      DTC = Direct Traffic Control/Manual Block (no CTC or block signals, this is "dark territory").
      CTC = Centralized Traffic Central (no block signals). See Page 42 and Page 43 of NC&STL's "What CTC Did for the Dixie Line" article from the
           February 7, 1955 issue of Railway Age Magazine.
      IHS = Interlocking Home Signals.
      ABS = Automatic Block Signals.
      The main line has no defect detectors and possibly used the jointed (bolted) rail all the way.
(D) NC&STL Rwy. April 29, 1951 Time Table No. 170, pg. 4 & 5. All speed are good for F, GP, J & K Class Engines except H & L-2 Class Engines.
      P = Passenger trains, F = Freight trains and M = Military impedimenta trains. Contributed by David Ellenburg.
(E) L&N Railroad Company Nashville Division, Time Table No. 9, pg. 8, December 14, 1975.
(F) Based on 55 feet per car.
(G) NC&STL Rwy. June 1, 1919 Time Table No. 12 Atlanta, Chattanooga, Nashville, Hollow Rock Jct., pg. 3 to 4.
(H) NC&STL Rwy. Winter 1955 Time Table No. 2 Atlanta, Nashville, Bruceton and Memphis, pg. 4.
