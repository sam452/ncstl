NCPS Reunion 2011 - Tullahoma

The Tullahoma Event Center will be the venue for the 2011 NCPS Reunion the weekend of May 21-23. Mark your calendars now to save the weekend!

The Reunion will include former employees (including Mark Womack), speakers, modeling clinics, modeling contest, displays, side trips to areas of interest, and the Annual NCPS Business Meeting.

Registration for the weekend will be $40 for members, $50 for non-members; $25 for spouse; $10 late fee after May 1;
Motor car ride is $40 additional fee (reservations closed for motor car ride)
Registration includes Friday and Saturday evening meals. 
Saturday box lunch is only included with the motorcar rides.

Hotel information will be sent after registration.  Hotels are packed for the weekend - and reservations must be made by May 7. Due to overscheduled hotels, we prefer not to post the discount information online.

NARCOA - Ride the rails in a motorcar!!!

We already have over 30 people signed up for the motorcar ride. Late registrations for the motorcar ride will be put on a wait list in case of cancellations or additional seats being offered.  Deposit will be refunded if your name has been wait listed and we do not get you on the ride.

National Association of Railcar Owners of America (NARCOA) will bring a number of railroad motorcars to Tullahoma the weekend of our 2011 Reunion. In conjunction with the Reunion, we will be offering to our Reunion attendees a ride on these motorcars on the morning of Saturday May 21st. This ride will begin in Tullahoma, Tennessee and will proceed up the old Sparta Branch of the NC&St.L, and culminate in Rock Island, Tennessee where we have lunch at a trackside restaurant. We will then bus back to Tullahoma. The trip including a stop for lunch should last around six hours. Please note that a trip on a motorcar is a loud, rough riding, dirty affair. Keep this in mind before signing up for this trip.

Because we are bringing these cars from as far away as North Carolina and because this event requires an extensive amount of planning, an advance non-refundable deposit of $40.00 was required, and reservations for the motor car rides closed January 1, 2011.

If for some reason we are unable to bring the motorcars to the Reunion, you will get a full refund of the motorcar deposit,
