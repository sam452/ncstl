GP7 AAR Switcher Truck Conversion

Here is what it takes to change the trucks on an Atlas™ Geep to AAR Switcher.

Folks, there is really not much to it: Fifteen easy steps! Atlas factory was mistaken about unsuitability of their switcher trucks, but it is not a drop-in. The finished result looks great and operates as new.

  1. Order/obtain the parts on the parts list. Cost will be about $28-30.

  2. Disassemble the switcher trucks with care.

  3. Using a 2-56 tap, tap out the metric bolster hole in the new trucks. Do not go all the way through. Leave the hole rough at the bottom to help hold the new bolster (kingpin) screws. Perfectly clean the truck of any shavings and reassemble them.

  4. Wash your hands. Remove the shell from the chassis.

  5. Disconnect the two wires to each truck from the top circuit board. Plastic clips pull off.

  6. Remove the existing Bloomberg trucks, (Snap off the top worm cover). Save all parts.

  7. Make a pair of spacer blocks with a numbered drill #44 hole in the center. Specs given.

  8. Use the same #44 drill to clean out the truck-locating hole in the frame at each end.

  9. Install the new trucks and check for proper coupler height. Assure kingpin screws do not project into gear case. Leave a little looseness in the screws. Fasten wires to Ckt. bd.

  10. Remove the universal joint from each Bloomberg worm shaft.

  11. Install the universals to the new worm gears; lubricate the worm and bearings. Make sure there is a little "endplay" in the shaft

  12. Assemble the worm and shafts into the new trucks and snap on the worm retainer.

  13. Test run the mechanism. Now is good time to add DCC if desired. Fix any problems.

  14. Reassemble the shell and handrails; use care handling with fragile side handrails.

  15. Phase two is cosmetic: change the numbers, add Pyle Gyralite detail if desired.

The toughest part is taking the new trucks apart and cleanly tapping an unfinished 2-56 pre-existing hole for the new bolster pin screw. No soldering required. Only two small truck adaptor spacers to make from scratch, I used styrene since it's available and easy to fabricate. One could use a stack of tiny washers. The fussiest part is cleanly changing the numbers. Take care to not damage either the spring contacts on the trucks or run the kingpin screw in too deeply (It will ruin the gears inside the truck!)

See the parts list: order a pair of Atlas Switcher trucks for the Alco S-2/4. These are the AAR variety. They will come to you without worm gear and shaft assemblies, nor the top retainer clip. Order these parts, too and include a few extra thrust washers. The washers are to install on the worm shafts between the worm and the bearings to minimize shaft endplay. A tiny bit is good.

Second phase of conversion:

Changing the numbers will require complete removal of the old numbers as the only close font in maroon is in the discontinued Microscale 87-901 NC&StL Diesel decal set and it is slightly more condensed. There may be a Railroad Roman number set in dark red available also. I gently scrape the numbers away a layer at a time using a hobby knife with a new blade. The yellow underneath is thick enough to take some abuse. Then apply the desired new number, 700 to 704.

Changing the number boards is tedious, careful detail work with the same hobby knife: the black coating is what describes the numbers. I recommend Testor's gloss enamel, "PLA" to blank out the six or seven there and hand scribing in a new number. Work under a well-lit magnifier and go really slow with the knife-edge scratching the black away to reveal the white underneath of the number you want. This will then show up as back-lit when the engine is running as it is supposed to.

The headlight change is a cut and fit affair. First remove the top hand grab on each end as they are in the way for fitting the new headlights. They just pull out of holes. Carve away the detail of the existing Pyle dual headlight. Dress the area with a good fine-cut file to flatten it, and glue on the predrilled Gyralite housings. I used a number 47-drill bit and Athearn Geep headlight Lucite inserts to make them still operate. Note that the spacing center to center of the new castings does not match the Atlas spacing. Take care to get them positioned right, as well as straight up and down. Superglue the replacement dual headlight housings (non working) right on the painted surface below the Gyralites as appropriate.

Touchup and paint the added headlight/Gyralites with a 1/3-1/3-1/3 mix of Floquil Boxcar Red, Caboose red, and Glaze. I suppose you could use other brands, leave out the glaze. Gently hand-twist a number 47-drill bit in the metal headlight recesses to polish up the reflectors. Add a tiny spot of Testor's Ruby Red or other translucent red to the second lens from the top as the warning light. Reinstall the two previously removed hand grabs, lightly pressed into their respective holes.


PARTS LIST

For Trucks Change:

  • Atlas™ AAR switcher trucks, one pair 827300
  • Atlas™ Worm and shaft assembly, 807306 (includes two bearings)
  • Atlas™ thrust washers, 807307
  • Atlas™ worm retainer clip 807309
  • Any #2-56X 5/16" machine screw, (2 ea) I prefer Phillips head. Good source is Kaydee™ coupler kits for a plastic screw if desired (easy to cut and fit but metal is best)
  • # 2 flat washers, 10 each if desired

Cosmetic Detail Changes:

  • Detail Assoc. Dual oscillating headlight, 1002
  • Details West HL-148
  • Athearn Geep 7 headlight lenses
  • Microscale NC&StL Diesel decal set 87-901or other dark red Railroad Roman Number set

TOOLS

  • Miniature Phillips screw driver
  • Needle nose tweezers
  • Flush-cutting diagonal cutters, fine
  • Hobby Knife with assortment of new blades
  • #2-56 tap, means to turn it accurately
  • 6" fine cut flat file
  • #44 drill bit
  • #47 drill bit
  • Small hobby paintbrush
  • Eye Loupe or magnifying glass (to do the number board change neatly.)

SUPPLIES

  • Superglue (Needed anyway to attach the cut levers!)
  • Plastic cement, liquid preferred
  • Decal film solvent such as Solvaset™
  • Labelle Gear lube 103
  • Styrene pieces to make ¼" by 3/16" by 7/32" truck spacers
  • Paints: Gloss black, Caboose Red, Boxcar Red, Glaze if Floquil™ is used

This should about do it for a neat conversion and upgrade in appearance to be nearly perfect for the NC's first order of Geeps. Enjoy!

Tom Knowles
