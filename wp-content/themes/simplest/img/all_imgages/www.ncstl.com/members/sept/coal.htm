Coal Mine - Tracy City Area

A Glance Back...

By: William Ray Turner, Grundy County Historian
from the Tullahoma News site

Can you stand to read some more about the early coal mines in the Grundy County area? Well, if you can, lets take another "Glance Back" to early mines in 1909.

There was a mine, "K Mine," located near Coalmont and the operator was The Chattanooga Mining Company, Chattanooga, Tennessee. The president of the company was W.E. Kunz of Chattanooga and the owner of the company was J.B. Ferguson of Coalmont. The average thickness of the coal in this mine was 30 inches. Mules pulled the cars of coal from the head of the entry to the tipple, a distance of 2000 feet. J.R. Ryan of Coalmont was the inside Foreman.

Another coal mine at Coalmont was known as "A Mine" and the owner and operator was the Sewanee Fuel and Iron Company of Coalmont. The president of the company in 1909 was F.B. Martin of Chattanooga and the inside foreman was J.M. Seahorn of Coalmont. The average thickness of the coal was thirty inches. Mules also pulled the coal from the mine rooms to the tipple, a distance of 2,500 feet.

This same company operated the coal mine at Coalmont known as "B Mine" and Mr. Seahorn of Coalmont was the inside foreman at this mine also. Coal was hauled from both of these coal mines by the Nashville, Chattanooga and St. Louis Railroad. From the rooms where the miners worked the coal , the mining mules pulled this coal a distance of 4,500 feet.

Another coal mine, The Brushy Ridge Mine, was the Nunley Ridge Coal Company's mine near Tracy City. The president of this company was E.L. Hampton of Tracy City and George M. Thorogood was the secretary with J.C. Roberts of Tracy City as inside foreman. This coal mine was about three miles from Tracy City and the coal was hauled from the mine by the Nashville, Chattanooga and St. Louis Railroad. The coal in this mine measured about 42 inches and the mules only had to pull this coal 400 feet from the main heading to the tipple. In the Nunley Ridge Coal Company Mine their daily output of coal in 1909 was just forty tons a day and at the Flat Branch Coal Company Mine it was 150 tons a day.

At the same time Tennessee Consolidated Coal Company was operating several mines in this area. The East Fork Mine was one of them. East Fork Coal Mine was located on the ridge above where the coke ovens were near the now Grundy Lakes. The president of this company was E.L. Hampton of Tracy City and R.B. -Roberts was the secretary. The inside foreman at East Fork was W.C. Parsons of Tracy City. The average thickness of this coal was 42 inches and a railroad ran to this mine from Tracy City, about one mile, it was the Nashville, Chattanooga and St. Louis Railroad. This East Fork Mine was originally owned by the Tennessee Coal, Iron and Railroad Company.
Tennessee Consolidated Coal Company also owned and operated several more coal mines in this area, the Ramsey Number 1 Mine, The Ramsey West Mine, Reid Hill Number 1, and The Street Hill Mine.

In 1909, I have only found two coal miners killed by a roof fall on September 23, 1909 at Reid Hill, Ben Gilley was killed on December 9, 1909 by a roof fall. Mr. Smith was single while Mr. Gilley was married.

There were three men injured in local coal mines in 1909 and all three were working in Mine B at Coalmont. They were James Anderson, W.B. Head and Will Smith. Coal minign was hard and dangerous work and someday I hope someone or a group will put up a monument to the coal miner. He sure deserves one.
