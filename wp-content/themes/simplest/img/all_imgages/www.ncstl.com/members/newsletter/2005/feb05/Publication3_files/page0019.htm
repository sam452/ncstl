Commonwealth designed Pullman trucks were up-dated and retained.

 

So we have six streamlined cars with welded sides and six wheel roller bearing drop-equalizer trucks with brake cylinders hung on their side frames. This was being done in 1946 in anticipation of a 1947 inaugural of the train.  By 1954 there were actually 10 City cars.

At about this time, parent company L&N needed to update some aging passenger cars and came up with a design for rebuilding. Taking some old ACF cars, they stripped them down to the window rail to look like streamliner lightweights, except they still featured the riveted belt rail and some vertical splices as seen on heavyweights.  From the windows up, the second batch resembled the NC's City of Memphis cars. These cars were in the L&N number series 2570 to 2582. Seating capacity was only 48.

The NC was taking their Pullman rebuilds down to the frames, a more expensive and time-consuming proposition. The original three coaches 1100 to 1102 for the City of Memphis (with a seating capacity of 56) "probably" became L&N 2589, 90 and 91 (p.168 Louisville & Nashville Passenger Trains by Castner, Chapman and Dorin).
