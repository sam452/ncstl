To remove the truck, slip a straight blade jeweler's screwdriver under the retaining clip for the worm gear on the top of the gear tower for each truck. Gently pry upward, and the clip should pop off, allowing the truck to fall out of the frame. The clip also retains the worm gears and bearings. Notice the new truck has a tapped hole in the center for the kingpin screw this is what retains it when we get that far.

 

4. Enlarge Bolster Pivot Point

Also observe there is a hole in the frame as a pivot point for the old truck. We will use that hole to mount the new truck, but it needs opened up to #44 drill size (Clearance for a #2 screw) so do that now.

 

5.  Disassemble Trucks

Disassemble the switcher trucks one at a time, by unscrewing the two small Phillips (metric) screws underneath. Pull the two halves apart and remove the center gear down at the wheels. Tap the existing center hole #2-56, but do not tap all the way through. Very important as this step will capture the mounting screw by interference and keep it secure when assembled. I did this step since I did not have any suitable 2 mm metric screws to mount the truck with.

 

When you are sure there are no chips left in the truck, reassemble it. Note that there are two phosphor bronze contacts that run on the inside of each wheel. These are the electrical contacts and quite fragile.

Take care to not bend them and if you do, they must be reformed to gently touch the inside of each wheel. The engine will not run well without care here.

 

6.  Make Truck Spacers

To make the truck spacer, I cut two pieces from Styrene ¼" square stock and glued that to a flat

of .020" styrene as a "foot". Cut them to fit so that each neatly sits on top of the truck. Drill a number 44 hole in the center of each one. Now we can trial fit the truck to the frame. Test and adjust by removing material as needed so that the coupler matches a known good height coupler or Kadee coupler gauge.

 

7.  Make retaining screws

This step is crucial to a good running engine: When satisfied with ride height, install the kingpin screw and gently run it in a little at a time testing to make sure it never touches the gears in the gear case. You can do this by turning the wheels by hand as you tighten the screw. If it drags the gears, remove the screw and file a little from its length, retry. If you force the screw in too deep it will ruin the gearbe VERY careful here. The truck should be able to swivel freely and have a little forward/back rocking motion available when the kingpin screw is run in as far as permitted by the gears. The wheels should easily turn when moved with your fingers, no binding.

 

8.  Reassemble Trucks

Remove the universal and short slip shafts from the Bloomberg trucks and press them on the Switcher worm shafts in a similar manner. Install the so-fitted worms into the gear towers and test for a tiny bit of endplay. If there is a lot, install a thrust washer one at a time till there is no more than about .002" endplay.
