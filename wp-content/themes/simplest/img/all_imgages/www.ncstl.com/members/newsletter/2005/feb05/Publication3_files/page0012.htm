paints. I use Floquil almost

exclusively, but dries very flat. To counter this and match the light sheen of the Atlas paint, add a little "Glaze" (clear). I settled on a 1/3-1/3-1/3 mix of Boxcar Red, Caboose red, and Glaze.  A tiny bit is needed, so I just mix a few drops (ten small drops of each color) in a throwaway plastic spoon and dab it on where needed with a brush. This dries to an exact match for the Atlas paint, which is to my eye a little "less maroon" than I remember as correct. It resembles the base color I might use for weathering a unit that’s been running for about five years with no washings… Just the same, it is quite nicely done, and will look great however it's used, weathered or not.  While at it, I touched up the brackets for the MU and signal hoses.

 

Conclusion

So that about does it for converting and upgrading the already very nice Atlas Geep to NC&StL practice, in the first order iteration. This will make a one-of a kind and distinctive looking engine that is perfectly at home on the main line or where it was intended, in branch line service. Better yet, it more closely preserves the memory of Grandpa's Road as it was into the Diesel age.

As usual, if there are any questions, feel free to contact me.
