Folks, there is really nothing to it. The toughest part is taking the new

trucks apart and cleanly tapping an unfinished 2-56 pre-existing hole for the #2-56 bolster screw. No soldering required. Only two small truck adaptor spacers to make from

scratch, I used styrene since it's available and easy to fabricate, but one could use a stack of number 2 flat washers on top of each truck.

 

1.         Order Parts

See the parts list: order a pair of Atlas Switcher trucks for the Alco S-2/4. These are the AAR variety. They will come to you without worm gear and shaft assemblies, nor the top retainer clip. Order these parts, too and include a few extra thrust washers. Go ahead and finish the assembly of the engine as it is shipped with end railings, cut levers and MU hoses not attached. Familiarize yourself with the way the engine is made. Atlas delivers parts in a very timely manner. The new trucks are finely detailed and well engineered. (See later notes on care when reassembling them though!)

 

2. Remove hood

When the parts come in, remove the shell from the chassis by releasing the rear side handrails from the cab and releasing the front side handrails from the front steps. Then gently squeeze the body amidships and rock the shell from the frame. It should come off easily.

Inside, you will see the simple beauty of the design, from the top circuit board (ckt. bd.) with two LEDs, to the precision motor and flywheels and tiny universals with slip-joints.

 

3. Disassemble chassis

Removing the Bloomberg trucks is a snap, literally. Notice the wires from the trucks go to the ckt. bd. and hide under a little black plastic nub in four places. These are retainers for the wires, and will slip off with some trouble. Do not loose

 

them! Remove all four wires, no de-soldering (or re-soldering either) needed!
